Raising Chickens: A Beginner's Guide

From fresh eggs to friendly pets, discover how raising chickens can transform your backyard and lifestyle.

Getting Started

Chickens have been domesticated for thousnds of years and are one of the more rewarding animals for homesteders and hobby farmers. Before you begin, check you local ordinances and plan a safe coop to protect them from other preditors.

"Raising chickens can be many things: therapeutic, rewarding, fun and for beginners maybe a bit nerve wrecking!" The Happy Chicken Coop

Start small, three to six hens are usually enough for a famil's egg supply and easier to manage for beginners.

Daily Care

Chickens do best when they are given a balanced diet with their feed, water, and room to run. Collecting the eggs daily and cleaning the coop clean monthly wil help keep your chickens healthy and happy. By giving them scratch grains and treats like mealworms will keep them engaged expecially in the winter months when they are cooped up more often.

Regular checks for signs of illness and providing dust baths will help control mites and other parasites.

Key Takeaways

  1. Start with a manaageable flock and a secure coop.
  2. Daily care includes feeding, watering, and cleaning.
  3. Chickens provide fresh eggs and enterntainment all year round.

Glossary

  • Brooder A heated enclosure used to raise chicks until they grow feathers.
  • Free-Range A method of raising chickens with outdoor access to forage naturally.
  • Layer A hen that is kept primarily for egg production.
