Favorite Adam Sandler Comedies

Summary: Summary: Happy Gilmore is a hockey player with an explosive temper who discovers a surprising talent for golf. After his grandmother’s house is repossessed, he joins a golf tournament to win enough money to buy it back. Though he has no etiquette for the sport, his raw power and passion make him a fan favorite. Along the way, he clashes with golf pro Shooter McGavin in one of the funniest rivalries in comedy. Happy’s unorthodox methods, like using hockey swings to hit golf balls, make him both a spectacle and a sensation. Beneath the chaos, the film delivers heart, showing Happy’s loyalty to his family and determination to prove himself. With its outrageous humor, fistfights on the green, and over-the-top moments, it became one of Sandler’s defining comedies. It’s ultimately a story about channeling anger into purpose and never underestimating the underdog.

Summary: In Hotel Transylvania, Adam Sandler voices Count Dracula, the owner of a hidden hotel designed as a sanctuary for monsters. After losing his wife to angry humans, Dracula becomes an overprotective father to his daughter, Mavis. Determined to keep her safe, he builds the hotel where monsters like Frankenstein, the Mummy, and the Invisible Man can relax far away from people. On Mavis’s 118th birthday, a human named Jonathan accidentally finds the hotel and disrupts everything. To Dracula’s horror, Mavis and Jonathan fall for each other, forcing him to face his fears and his prejudices. The film mixes hilarious moments with heartfelt lessons about love and acceptance. Through colorful animation and fast-paced humor, it shows that even monsters have feelings and families just like humans do. In the end, Dracula learns that letting go is part of loving—and that the human world isn’t as scary as he thought.

The movie delivers a heartwarming message about love, acceptance, and letting go of overprotectiveness — all wrapped in hilarious monster mayhem.

Summary: Grown Ups follows five childhood friends—Lenny, Eric, Kurt, Marcus, and Rob—who reunite after the death of their beloved basketball coach. Now middle-aged and each facing their own struggles, they gather with their families at a lake house for a summer weekend. Lenny, played by Adam Sandler, is a successful Hollywood agent trying to reconnect with his kids and his roots. The group relives their youth through games, jokes, and ridiculous competitions that prove age is just a number. As they spend more time together, they realize that true happiness doesn’t come from success or fame but from friendship and family. The movie balances comedy and heart, showcasing the importance of laughter and nostalgia. From water park antics to hilarious parenting fails, it celebrates the chaos of growing older without growing up. In the end, Grown Ups reminds audiences that being surrounded by the people you love makes life worth living.
